The usual AI-writing loop is copy the text, switch to a browser or a chat app, paste, wait, copy the result, switch back, around 45 seconds of context switching for a one-line edit. Camus collapses that whole loop into a single keystroke, inside whatever app you're already writing in. Select text anywhere on macOS, press Ctrl+Shift+C, and Camus pops up with six presets (Improve, Shorten, Friendly, Reply, Summarize, LinkedIn, GitHub Issue) plus custom instructions. Pick one and the rewrite replaces your text or copies to your clipboard instantly.

Every rewrite is saved to a searchable history, including the original draft, across 20+ applications. It's built for anyone who writes across email, Slack, docs, and social media all day and wants better prose without leaving the app they're in. An offline mode runs a local model (Gemma 3n) for anything you'd rather not send anywhere. There's a 7-day free trial with no credit card, then Camus Pro is $3.99/month for the first three months, $7.99/month after.
