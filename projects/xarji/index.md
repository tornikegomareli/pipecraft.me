Not one Georgian bank exposes a public transaction API, and the native banking apps don't give you custom categories, advanced filters, or budget limits. Xarji is a self-hosted personal finance dashboard for Georgia that reads the bank transaction SMS macOS already mirrors from your iPhone into Messages.app's local SQLite database, parses them locally, and turns them into six dashboard screens: overview, transactions, categories, merchants, signals, and settings. Nothing is uploaded, and your own InstantDB instance handles sync.

It's built for macOS users on Apple Silicon in Georgia with a Bank of Georgia or TBC account today, with Liberty, Basis, ProCredit, Credo, Terabank, Cartu, Halyk, VTB Georgia, PASHA, Isbank, Ziraat, and Silk Road on the roadmap. Free, forever, fully open source under MIT, and it works offline after the first read.
